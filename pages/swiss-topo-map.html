Swiss Topo Map

Oct 7, 2024


Recently Ive been fascinated by old atlases, specifically Eduar Imhof's classroom atlases and The Great Geographic Atlas by Rand McNally from the 1980s I bought at a thriftstore a while ago. I learn best by doing, so I thought attempting to replicate one of these map would be good practice where I'd learn a lot in the process. The map I chose was Eduard Imhof's Overview Map of Switzerland from his Middle School Atlas.

I really like the way manual shaded relief look so for this map I used Terrain Sculptor to attempt to generalized my terrain similar to manual relief. Then I draped the hypsometric tint on it using similar colors to Imhof's from QGIS. For almost all of the map featured I using EuroGeographic datasets. Their data was lacking mountain peaks however, so I got those from OpenStreetMaps.

Tools: QGIS, Terrain Sculptor, GIMP, Inkscape
Data Sources: SRTM, EuroGeographic, OpenStreetMaps
